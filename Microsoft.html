Microsoft








Symbol: MSFT
Exchange: NYSE

Business Model: To enable people and organizations throughout the world to do more and achieve more by creating technology that transforms the way people learn, work, play, and communicate.

Competitive Advantage: Gives customers choice, value, flexibility, security, and compatibility with a broad range of hardware and software applications. Softwares are entrenched in business and institutional world.

P/E Current: 17.52
14-yr Range: 9.73 – 36.44

Market Cap: $368.51 Billion
Shares outstanding: 8.09 Billion

Current Price: (July 13th 2015) 45.54 at close
12-month forecast: $62.23 – 82.84

Rating: Undervalue/Buy

Key Facts:

Additional Factors:

Sales growth is on a upward trend since 2009, it reached 11.54% in 2014.

The firm is restructuring to refocus on producing useful software and finding new ways to generate profits in the software licensing industry. This can be seen in the new Office 365 service.

Microsoft CEO Nadella has made an effort to undo the company's aquisition of Nokia by writing off $7.5 billion dollars from the aquisition and conducting massive lay-offs. This allows Nokia to be easily incorporated into the company's Devices & Services Hardware division. The company will have reduced tax liabilities while retaining ownership of the Lumia phone line.

Additional Factors:

Gross margins are decreasing as debt levels increase. The company will need to manage its capital well as interest rates get set to rise, especially in times of restructuring.
